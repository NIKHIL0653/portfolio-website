More Fuel for the Fire: Expanding the Dataset

My Azerbaijan model was a good start, but I knew that to make more accurate predictions, I needed more data. So, for the Singapore GP model, I went back to the FastF1 library and gathered even more information for every race from 2016 to 2024. This time, in addition to lap times, sector times, and qualifying results, I also collected detailed weather data for each session.

As any F1 fan knows, weather can be a huge factor in a race. Rain can turn a procession into a lottery, and even small changes in temperature can affect a car's performance. By adding weather data to my model, I was giving it a much more complete picture of the race conditions.

A More Sophisticated Engine: Introducing XGBoost

For my Azerbaijan model, I used a Gradient Boosting Regressor. It's a solid model, but I knew I could do better. So, for the Singapore GP, I upgraded to an XGBoost Regressor.

Think of XGBoost as a high-performance engine for machine learning. It's known for its speed and accuracy, and it's a popular choice for winning machine learning competitions. By switching to XGBoost, I was giving my model the power it needed to handle a more complex dataset and make even more accurate predictions.

Fine-Tuning the Setup: Advanced Feature Engineering

With a more powerful model and a richer dataset, I could also get more creative with my feature engineering. Here are some of the key improvements I made:

  • Detailed Weather Features: Instead of just using placeholders for rain and temperature, I now had real data for AirTemp, Humidity, Pressure, Rainfall, TrackTemp, WindDirection, and WindSpeed. This allows the model to learn how different weather conditions affect lap times at the Marina Bay circuit.
  • Circuit-Specific Features: The Singapore GP is a unique track, and I wanted my model to understand that. So, I added features for CircuitLength and NumberOfCorners, as well as a CircuitType feature to distinguish it from other tracks.
  • Team Performance Score: I refined my TeamPerformanceScore to better reflect the current hierarchy in F1. This helps the model to understand the relative strengths of the different teams on the grid.

The Moment of Truth: Predicting the Singapore GP

After all the hard work, it was time to put my new model to the test. I created a script to predict the top 10 finishers for a hypothetical 2025 Singapore Grand Prix, and the results were very promising.

The model was able to make nuanced predictions, taking into account all of the new features I had added. It's still not a crystal ball, but it's a big step forward from my Azerbaijan model.
